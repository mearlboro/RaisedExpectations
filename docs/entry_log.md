## Monday July 13
- Discussed research question ideas, specifically thinking about the role of participant familiarity on accuracy of guessing attempts and on the clue giver's behaviour
- Discussed individual competencies and loosely allocated roles and tasks for the coming week
- Wrote initial paragraph on research idea and plan:
> Dyadic social interactions unfold differently depending on whether interactors are familiar to each other or not. In this project, we investigate the effect of familiarity on full body movement dynamics, speech acoustics, and speech fluency. We focus on a subset of the data from the balance corpus in the unstable condition (clue giver is standing on the wobble board). We are particularly interested in the effect of familiarity on the dynamics of (i) body movements, (ii) speech acoustics, and (iii) speech fluency of the clue giver, depending on whether the guesser to whom their communication is directed is a friend or a stranger. As a potential extension, we also want to look at the tangibility of the target words in the Taboo game.
